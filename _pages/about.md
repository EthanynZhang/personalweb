I am a full-time PhD student at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, supervised by [Prof. Di Zou](https://www.polyu.edu.hk/engl/people/academic-staff/prof-di-zou/). My research examines how Generative AI and Virtual Reality can support second-language learning, especially through embodied conversational agents for spoken L2 practice.

My current work centers on **pedagogical fidelity in GenAI**: how agent personalities, teaching strategies, feedback behaviors, and embodied interaction can align with real language-learning goals. I build AI-driven learning experiences with game engines, real-time rendering technologies, and XR systems, then evaluate how these systems shape learner engagement, confidence, and performance.

Before joining PolyU, I completed a Master of Engineering at Peking University and a Bachelor of Engineering at Shandong University. My background spans education technology, HCI, VR/AR/XR, game-based learning, serious games, AI product work, and interactive media systems.

I am broadly interested in AI in education, VR/AR/XR in education, Computer-Assisted Language Learning (CALL), Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and game-based learning. I especially value research where technical systems are more than demonstrations: they help learners practice, communicate, and participate in more humane learning environments.
