    Educational Outreach Volunteer Work

    Volunteer teaching team leader, Teach for China and community education outreach, 2021

    As a volunteer teaching team leader, I participated in community-based teaching and educational support activities in Urad Front Banner, Bayannur, Inner Mongolia. The work included organizing volunteer teaching sessions, supporting local students, and helping design approachable learning activities during school breaks.

    Computer Animation

    Teaching assistant, Peking University, 2021

    As a teaching assistant for Computer Animation at Peking University, I supported students working with animation concepts, programming exercises, and visual computing foundations. The role strengthened my ability to explain technical ideas in a way that students could connect to visible outcomes: motion, timing, transformation, interaction, and visual expression.

    Popular Science and Learning Methods

    Outreach lecturer, Hailiang Education, 2023

    At Hailiang Education, I delivered outreach lectures on popular science and learning methods. These sessions were designed for young learners and public audiences who needed educational content that was clear, encouraging, and connected to everyday experience.

    AIGC and Game Development

    Student lecturer, Geek Lab, Peking University, 2024

    As a student lecturer at Geek Lab of Peking University, I taught Unity, AIGC, and project-based creative technology to students interested in games, interactive media, and AI-assisted production. The course was designed around hands-on making: students learned core game-engine workflows, explored generative AI tools, and gradually turned early ideas into playable or demonstrable prototypes.

    Art of Debate

    Teaching assistant, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, 2025

    As a teaching assistant for Art of Debate at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, I supported students in developing argumentation, public speaking, and structured discussion skills. The course connects closely with my broader interest in spoken language practice: effective communication involves timing, evidence, confidence, audience awareness, and social presence.
